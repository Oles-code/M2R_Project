## Caveats (read alongside any quoted number)

- **CRISPRi ≠ deletion**: every knockdown is `do(x_i = low)`, a *soft* intervention. The direction of the predicted shift is meaningful but the magnitude is not a calibrated SEM effect size.

- **Out-of-support (Schultheiss §5)**: knocked-down cells often sit outside the observational support, so the comparison probes an extrapolation regime. Treat the Wasserstein magnitudes as a ranking, not a fitted effect.

- **Multiple testing**: ~p² Mann–Whitney tests were Benjamini–Hochberg adjusted at α = 0.05. The CSV exposes both raw and adjusted p-values.

- **Self-edges**: ordered pairs (i, i) are never tested — knocking out i trivially shifts i.

- **Small environments**: pairs with fewer than 50 interventional cells for the cause gene are flagged `underpowered` and excluded from the confirmed-edge and false-omission rates.

- **No-direct-edge ≠ no-path**: a zero in B is only a claim of no *direct* edge. Indirect paths i → k → j would still shift j; the false-omission test deliberately samples only ordered pairs with no directed path of any length in the recovered DAG.
